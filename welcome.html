Before Your First Lesson

What to have ready
on lesson day.

Lessons are delivered via Google Meet. No account or download is required for your child. Here is everything to have in place before the lesson starts.

A device with camera and microphone
A laptop, tablet, or desktop works well. A phone screen is small for extended reading work. A headset or earbuds can help with audio clarity but are not required.
A stable internet connection
A wired or strong wifi connection is ideal. If the connection drops during a lesson, rejoin the Google Meet link and the lesson continues from where it left off.
A quiet space
Your child should be in a space where they can focus without distraction. A bedroom, study, or quiet corner works well. Background noise can make it harder for both your child and their specialist to hear clearly.
The items from your BSL Lit Kit
The whiteboard, marker, eraser, pencil, pencil grip, and notebook from your kit are used in lessons. Have them within reach before the lesson starts. The pop-it can be on the desk too.
Your Google Meet link
Your lesson link is in your Paperbell confirmation and in the 24-hour reminder email. Click it a minute or two before your lesson time. Your specialist will be there waiting.
A relaxed child, and a parent nearby
No preparation needed and nothing to study. Let your child know they are going to meet someone who is looking forward to getting to know them. You do not need to sit in the lesson, but stay close enough that your child knows you are there. That quiet presence makes a difference, especially in the early lessons.
How Lessons Work

The Bright Spiral
Literacy Method.

Every lesson follows the same four-step structure. The content changes with every lesson. The structure stays consistent because that consistency is part of what makes the method work.

Ground
Assess the whole child
Every lesson begins by connecting with your child, reviewing what came before, and checking in before instruction starts. Relationship comes first.
Build
Evidence-based lessons
Targeted instruction on the specific skills identified in your child's assessment. Structured, sequential, and built around exactly where they are.
Rise
Track measurable growth
Progress is visible and documented at every stage. You always know what is improving, what comes next, and how your child is moving forward.
Shine
Confidence that lasts
The goal is a child who sees themselves as a reader and a writer. That identity is built lesson by lesson, and it carries into the classroom and beyond.
After lessons: how we keep you informed
Dr. Overstreet or a member of her team will be in touch after a lesson when there is something worth flagging: a notable breakthrough, a shift in approach, or something specific to watch for at home. You will also receive written updates at the key milestones included in your package. If you ever want to know how a specific lesson went, email hello@brightspiralliteracy.com and you will hear back within 48 hours on weekdays.
Your BSL Lit Kit

What is in your kit
and why it is there.

The BSL Lit Kit is included with Complete and Signature packages. Every item in it has a specific purpose inside a structured literacy lesson. This is not a gift bag. It is the toolkit your child will use from lesson one.

🟦
Whiteboard
Used for sound-mapping, spelling work, and word-building. Writing and erasing on a whiteboard removes the permanence pressure that pencil and paper can create.
✏️
Marker & Eraser
Dry-erase marker for the whiteboard. The eraser makes it easy to try again without any trace of the attempt. That matters more than it sounds.
📝
Pencil & Pencil Grip
For notebook work and writing tasks. The pencil grip supports proper pencil hold, which affects fluency and stamina for children who are still developing fine motor control.
📒
Notebook
Your child's BSL notebook stays with them across lessons. It builds a visible record of words learned, patterns practiced, and progress made. Seeing a notebook fill up matters to a child.
🔵
Pop-it
A tactile focus tool. Some children think and process better when their hands are doing something. The pop-it gives them something quiet and low-distraction to engage with during listening or thinking moments in a lesson. It is not a toy in this context. It is a tool.
Your BSL Lit Kit has been dispatched and is on its way to you. If it has not arrived within 10 days of your purchase, reach out at hello@brightspiralliteracy.com and we will sort it out.
