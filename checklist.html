What to Notice

A parent's checklist for reading and literacy at home

You know your child better than anyone. And sometimes that means noticing things that are hard to name. Maybe reading feels like a battle every night. Maybe your child seems fine but something feels off. Maybe their teacher says not to worry, and you're still worried.

This checklist won't diagnose anything. What it will do is give you a clearer picture of what you're actually seeing, so you can trust your instincts and know what questions to ask.

📖
At homework time
Writing assignments get started late, kept short, or avoided entirely
Spelling looks inconsistent, the same word spelled differently in the same paragraph
They erase constantly, or refuse to write anything they're not sure about
Reading assignments take much longer than they seem like they should
🌙
During bedtime reading
They guess at unfamiliar words using the first letter or the picture rather than working through it
They can read the words but struggle to tell you what just happened
They rush, or find reasons to stop early
They'll listen to a story happily but resist reading one themselves
✏️
When they write anything down
Letters or words reversed beyond early first grade
Sentences are short and simple even when they can talk about the same topic in detail
They know what they want to say but it doesn't come out on the page
💬
How they talk about reading and school
"I'm not a reader" said with real feeling, not just preference
They were managing fine in earlier grades and now things feel harder
They get frustrated with tasks that peers seem to handle easily
They describe themselves as bad at school, or have stopped talking about it

If two or more of these feel familiar, that's not something to wait out. Reading gaps at this age are very responsive to the right support, and the earlier you have a clear picture, the easier it is to act on it.
