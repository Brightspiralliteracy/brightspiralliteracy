About

Dr. Jenn Overstreet

Dr. Jenn Overstreet has spent 15 years inside international school classrooms across the US, Italy, and Saudi Arabia, teaching children to read and training other teachers in evidence-based literacy methods. That second part matters. Working at the level of teacher training means understanding not just how to reach individual children, but why so many instructional approaches fail to reach them in the first place.

What she has seen across every school, every curriculum, and every context is the same pattern: when a child is not reading, the instruction has not found them yet. That is not a flaw in the child. It is a gap in the system. Bright Spiral Literacy exists because that gap is solvable, and because internationally mobile families deserve access to the kind of specialist who understands both the science of reading and the specific world their child is growing up in.

Her doctorate in psychology grounds her in the cognitive science of how children develop reading and language. Her K-12 Special Education license, school principal credential, and instructional coaching certification reflect the full range of what she has been trusted to do inside schools. Her 15 years in IB, Cambridge, and American curriculum environments inform everything that happens in a BSL lesson.

The method
Structured literacy is an evidence-based approach to teaching reading that is explicit, systematic, and cumulative. It teaches the building blocks of reading in a specific sequence: phonemic awareness (hearing the individual sounds in words), phonics (connecting sounds to letters), fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. This is different from the balanced literacy approach, which relies heavily on guessing from context and picture cues. The research on structured literacy is clear and consistent: it works for the widest range of learners, including children who have not responded to other approaches. It is not a new idea. It is what the science of reading has supported for decades.
Every lesson is built on the Bright Spiral Literacy Method: Ground, Build, Rise, Shine. We begin by connecting with your child and reviewing what came before, then move into targeted instruction on the specific skills their assessment identified. Lessons are structured and purposeful, but not rigid or mechanical. The pace is set by the child. Within 48 hours of each lesson, you receive a debrief on what was covered and what to expect next. Lessons are 45 minutes, one-on-one, and delivered online via Google Meet.
Where it helps, short practice activities between lessons are suggested. These are always simple and manageable, not a second layer of homework stress. The debrief after each lesson will let you know what, if anything, would be useful to do before the next one. Nothing is mandatory and nothing requires you to be a reading expert at home.
Bright Spiral Literacy works with children in Kindergarten through Grade 5, roughly ages 5 to 11. Within that range, lessons are fully tailored to where your child actually is as a reader, not just their grade level.

How your data is used

Who we are

Children's data

Any information about a child shared with us by a parent or guardian is treated with the highest level of confidentiality and used only to deliver our literacy services.

Your rights under GDPR

If you are based in the EU, you have the right to access, correct, or request deletion of any personal data we hold about you. You also have the right to object to processing and to data portability. To exercise any of these rights, contact us at hello@brightspiralliteracy.com. We will respond within 30 days.

Data retention

Email correspondence is retained for as long as necessary to deliver our services and meet any legal obligations. Client records are kept for a maximum of three years after the end of the service relationship, after which they are deleted.
